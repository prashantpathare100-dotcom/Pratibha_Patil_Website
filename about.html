About Pratibha Patil

A life of service, courage, and determination

Early Life and Education

Pratibha Devisingh Patil was born on December 19, 1934, in Nadgaon village, Maharashtra. She pursued her education at Mooljee Jetha College, Jalgaon, and later at Government Law College, Mumbai, where she earned her law degree.

Starting her career as a lawyer, she quickly became involved in social work and public service, driven by a deep commitment to justice and equality.

Political Journey

Her political career began in 1962 when she was elected to the Maharashtra Legislative Assembly. Over the decades, she held various ministerial portfolios and was elected to the Lok Sabha and Rajya Sabha, representing the people with dedication.

Values & Principles

Justice

Advocated for equal rights and legal reforms for women and marginalized communities.

Service

Dedicated her life to public service and social welfare initiatives.

Empowerment

Championed education and healthcare as tools for women's empowerment.

Integrity

Maintained strong ethical standards throughout her public life.

Constitutional Respect

Upheld the dignity of democratic institutions and emphasized constitutional values in public life.

Education

Promoted education as the foundation for social progress, inclusion, and opportunity for all.

What She Represented

Pratibha Patil stood for inclusivity, social justice, and the empowerment of women. Her presidency symbolized the maturing of Indian democracy and the breaking of gender barriers at the highest level.

Pratibha Patil
